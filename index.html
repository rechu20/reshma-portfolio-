Academic & Lab Research

Neural Engineering

EMG-Based NLARX Modelling for Task Performance Assessment

Developed an 8-unit Wavelet NLARX pipeline mapping EMG to wrist dynamics. Demonstrated that temporal NLARX residuals serve as a unique subject-specific signal modifier, improving Extension task pass/fail prediction by 9.8% and Alternating tasks by 7.0% compared to raw EMG amplitude.

Accuracy: Up to 88.9% within-subject, 81.4% cross-subject task discrimination
Fit Quality: Flexion task R=0.937, Extension task R=0.903 (Order [7,7,4])
Highlights: Identified coactivation index prominence (11.40% importance in pooled cohort)
Status: Manuscript in prep for Frontiers (2026)
